Query expansion [7, 19, 178, 213] and rewriting [143, 159, 277] aim to improve retrieval by refor- mulating the input query. Roy et al. [213] expanded queries with semantically related terms using word2vec [172]; Naseri et al. [178] applied BERT to contextualize expansion based on retrieved docu- ments. Ma et al. [159] trained LMs to rewrite queries using initial retrieval outputs. Jiang et al. [92] introduced DeepRetrieval, an RL- based approach that optimizes retrieval metrics (e.g., Recall@K, NDCG) directly without supervision, and achieves SOTA perfor- mance across literature, evidence-seeking, and SQL retrieval tasks with only 3B parameters. Experimental results show that effective query rewriting/expansion significantly improves retrieval quality. Building on this line, s3 [96] further demonstrates that training a search-only agent with reinforcement learning—using a generation- aware reward rather than search-only metrics—can yield strong downstream improvements while requiring orders of magnitude less training data. 3.2.4 Multi-staged Retrieval and Reranking. For efficiency and effec- tiveness, reranking processes [27, 35, 60, 118, 182] adopt two-stage retrieval strategies, where documents from a fast method are re- scored by a more powerful but slower ranking model. Nogueira and Cho [182] demonstrated BERT-based rerankers dramatically improve ranking quality. As NLP advances, LLMs [17, 206, 239] have emerged as state-of-the-art rerankers, boosting retrieval per- formance by large margins on both general [27, 35, 60, 118] and specific domains [3]. 4 Text Structuring Text structuring is essential for converting unstructured text into or- ganized, interpretable knowledge for analysis and decision-making. This section explores three key text mining methodologies: tax- onomy construction and enrichment, text classification, and infor- mation extraction. These approaches effectively organize textual information, enabling downstream tasks like knowledge graph con- struction, database population, and tabular relational learning. 4.1 Text Mining 4.1.1 Taxonomy. Taxonomy is a tree-like structure that organizes concepts into a hierarchical framework, where parent nodes rep- resent broader categories, and child nodes capture finer-grained subcategories. Taxonomy is often used as the label space structuring method for text-mining tasks. Taxonomy Construction. Taxonomy construction typically be- gins with a seed structure. HiExpan [224] expands entity taxonomies through width and depth expansions, followed by global structure adjustment using word analogy [202]. CoRel [81] leverages pre- trained language models (PLMs) to train a relation-transferring module that generalizes seed parent-child relationships. TaxoCom [120] completes partial topical taxonomies by learning local discrimina- tive word embeddings and applying novelty-adaptive clustering. A related task is set expansion, which extends a seed set with semantically similar entities.
