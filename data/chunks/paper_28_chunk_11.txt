When selecting a large language model (LLM) for deployment, it is essential to begin with a thorough evaluation of models that have been benchmarked on recognized datasets. Resources such as the Open LLM Leaderboard [33] and survey research papers [34] provide valuable insights into the performance and suitability of different LLMs for speciﬁc use cases. However, the selection process must also consider the organization’s resource constraints and policies. Critical deci- sions include whether to use locally hosted models like LLaMA or public API models like ChatGPT, as well as whether to opt for open-source models or proprietary solutions. The size of the model and the choice between versions should be guided by the available infrastructure for hosting, the cost associated with token usage, and the overall billing rates. While larger models generally excel at complex reasoning tasks, smaller models that have been ﬁne-tuned can often de- liver superior performance for speciﬁc tasks [35].
