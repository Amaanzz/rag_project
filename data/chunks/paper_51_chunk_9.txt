Knowledge Graph Construction. Knowledge graphs (KGs) rep- resent real-world entities as nodes and their relationships as edges. In the general domain, open information extraction (OpenIE) tools extract triples from raw documents, which are then filtered, linked, and merged to form KGs. However, pipelined methods often suffer from error propagation, leading to the development of end-to-end approaches. For instance, Zeng et al. [283] propose a sequence- to-sequence model that jointly extracts entities and relations, ad- dressing the overlap problem through a copy mechanism. Similarly, frameworks like REBEL [83] and ABSA [271] leverage pre-trained language models (PLMs) to translate raw text into structured knowl- edge. TagReal [89] uses pattern mining to generate high-quality prompts for KG completion. With the rise of large language models (LLMs), new methods for KG construction have emerged. Knowl- edgeGraph GPT [235] directly prompts GPT-4 to convert plain text into KGs. AutoKG [312] employs multiple LLM agents to enhance KG construction and reasoning. KG-FIT [90] refines KG embed- dings by constructing coherent entity hierarchies and fine-tuning them using both hierarchical and textual information. Domain- specific KG construction requires substantial human input for anno- tations and ontology development. For example, Rotmensch et al. [212] integrates annotated medical records with existing KGs to build a health-related KG. PAIR [59] uses LLMs as relation filters to streamline relation discovery in online marketing.
