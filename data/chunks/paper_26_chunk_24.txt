It is important to note that in this context, “Training” encompasses both initial model training and fine-tuning processes. 8 Comparisons of RAG 8.1 The Comprehensive Summary of RAG Table 2 presents a detailed analysis of the RAG studies discussed in this paper. The analysis shows that the majority of these studies have utilized external data sources to enrich the content of LLMs. A preference for multiple-hop over single-hop retrieval was noted, indicating that iterative search rounds generally yield superior results. In other words, most methods employ dense retrieval to secure higher quality candidate documents. Compared to modifying datasets in the pre-retrieval stage, more studies focus on manipulating the query to improve retrieval performance. Additionally, there is a significant emphasis on optimizing the retrieval phase, highlighting its crucial role in the research. However, there seems to be a scarcity of studies concentrating on customization in the generation stage, pointing to this as a potential area for future exploration. Overall, while the goal of RAG is to enhance the response quality of LLMs, greater efforts have been directed towards improving retrieval aspects. , Vol. 1, No. 1, Article .
