A related task is set expansion, which extends a seed set with semantically similar entities. SetExpan [223] uses skip-grams and word embeddings to measure similarity between candidates and the seed set. Set-CoExpan [80] introduces auxiliary sets through embedding learning. CGExpan [298] exploits PLMs to generate knowledge-probing queries, while ProbExpan [137] refines entity representations using contrastive learning. FGExpan [263] focuses on fine-grained set expansion, targeting specific common types. Taxonomy Enrichment. Taxonomy enrichment enhances each taxonomy node with discriminative texts, such as keywords. It can be classified into flat and hierarchical enrichment. Flat enrich- ment methods often use PLM embeddings for clustering [169, 302] or discriminative topic modeling. CatE [164] creates a joint word embedding space, while KeyETM [72] extends embedded topic modeling [45] by integrating topical keywords. GTM [36] focuses on guided topic modeling for short text. JoSH [170] leverages a taxonomy to train a joint embedding space that pre- serves relative tree distances between nodes. 4.1.2 Text Classification. Text classification categorizes text into different categories. According to how the label space is organized, it can be divided into flat classification (independent categories) and hierarchical classification (tree-structured labels). Flat Classification. Flat classification has been extensively studied. Early works [272, 294] focused on designing task-specific objectives for different architectures. Later, external supervision sources such as knowledge bases [228], human-curated rules [8, 20, 208], and keywords [163, 165, 187, 209] were utilized. Keywords inspired self-training methods like WeSTClass [165], LOTClass [168], and X-Class [255]. ClassKG [289] combined keywords and knowledge bases by constructing a keyword graph using GNNs. With the rise of LLMs, prompting-based methods [71, 245, 260, 313] achieved notable results by leveraging LLMs’ internal knowl- edge. Min et al. [173] proposed channel models for few-shot learn- ing, estimating conditional probabilities. NPPrompt [305] intro- duced a zero-shot approach that builds class verbalizers using PLM embeddings. PIEClass [296] explored discriminative PLM prompt- ing for pseudo-labeling. CARP [232] leveraged LLMs’ reasoning abilities for zero and few-shot classification, using CoT prompting to identify indicative clues before labeling. Hierarchical Classification. Hierarchical text classification as- signs input text to one or more nodes within a taxonomy-structured label space, presenting a greater challenge than flat classification due to its extensive structure. Most studies adopt either fully-supervised or semi-supervised approaches. Banerjee et al. [9] and Wehrmann et al. [257] train multiple classifiers for each node or level of the taxonomy.
