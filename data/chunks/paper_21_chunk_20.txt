Multilingual LLMs in general encounter challenges such as uneven distribution of languages in training corpora, or decline in performance for low-resource languages. A popular approach to deal with this problem has been to employ parameter tuning align- ment at multiple stages (Qin et al., 2024). However, it still remains as a challenge to handle language heterogeneity of signiﬁcantly varying syntax, morphology, and semantics (Xu et al., 2024), and our results seem to further reinforce the claim that there may be an underlying limitation to con- ventional approaches, without accounting for fundamental differences between the language systems. 11
