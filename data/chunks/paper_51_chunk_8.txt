WeSHClass [166] extends WeSTClass [165] by modeling label hierarchies as mixtures of distributions and using self-training. HiMeCat [295] integrates metadata by learning joint representa- tions for labels, metadata, and text using a hierarchical generative model, which supports weak supervision through augmented doc- uments. TaxoClass [222] leverages a pre-trained entailment model and employs taxonomy-guided search for pseudo-training samples. TELEClass [299] enhances taxonomies with class-indicative fea- tures, adapting LLMs for hierarchical classification by selecting pseudo labels from retrieved candidates and generating pseudo documents for long-tail and fine-grained classes. 4.1.3 Information Extraction. Information extraction is another important task for text mining, focusing on entity-level information as building blocks for knowledge structuring. Entity Mining. Entity mining includes named entity recognition (NER) and fine-grained entity typing (FET). NER involves extracting entities from text before recognition. Early approaches addressed uncertainty in distantly supervised labels: AutoNER’s "Tie or Break" strategy [218], PaTTER’s dictionary expansion [250], and ETAL’s confident entity identification [22]. RoSTER [167] addressed incom- plete labels using noise-robust learning. SDNET [25] pre-trained on Wikipedia entities. SEE-Few [273] utilized an entailment framework for learning from limited examples. Recent LLM approaches include GPT-NER [247] with self-verification, Shang et al. [220]’s document graphs, and X-NER [197] for extremely weakly supervised settings. FET deals with a more detailed label space organized into an on- tology. ZOE [307] utilized a taxonomy based on Freebase types. OntoType [116] aligned fine-grained types with an ontology using weak supervision. OnEFET [192] enriched ontology structures with instances. ALIGNIE [79] employed prompt-based approaches for interpreting entity types. SEType [301] incorporated additional en- tities from unlabeled corpora. Ultra-fine entity typing (UFET) [34] expanded the label space to thousands of types. BERT-MLMET[38] fine-tuned BERT with supervision from headwords and hypernyms. LITE [126] leveraged natural language inference for entity typing. DenoiseFET [132] clustered the extensive label space into centroids for better generalization. Relation Extraction. Building on mined entities, relation extrac- tion (RE) identifies relationships between entity pairs, playing a crucial role in knowledge graph construction (Sect. 4.2) and event induction [101, 109, 225]. Leveraging LLMs, Wadhwa et al. [243] demonstrate that few-shot prompting in GPT-3 can rival state-of-the-art fully supervised RE models and explore LLM-based data augmentation with CoT explanations. Wan et al. [244] en- hance in-context learning by utilizing task-aware sentence-level and entity-level representations for kNN retrieval of examples. Re- lationPrompt [32] prompts LLMs to synthesize relation examples for generating structured texts. To minimize hallucinations and enhance factual accuracy, STAR [157] and DocGNRE [129] adopt self-refinement and self-validation strategies. REPaL [309] suggests that relation descriptions offer broader semantic coverage than mere relation names.
