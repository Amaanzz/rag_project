Any budget-feasible combination contains at most DB = min{N, ⌊B/s⌋} chunks. (1) Time Complexity: Each of the I iterations performs selec- tion, expansion, simulation, and backpropagation. (i) Selection traverses O(DB) levels with O(N) children per level, yielding O(DB · N) complexity; (ii) Expansion creates up to N children, contributing O(N); (iii) Simulation evaluates all ex- panded children in a single batched reranker call, costing O(r); (iv) Backpropagation updates O(N) children across O(DB) levels, giving O(DB · N). Thus, the total time complexity is O(I · (DB · N + r)). A smaller B reduces DB, shrinking the search space. In practice, I is predicted by the configuration agent. As shown in Fig. 9, I remains a small value, ensuring efficient search. (2) Space Complexity: Space is allocated to store the nodes of the tree. In the i-th iteration, we expand ki nodes, where ki ≤ N. Over I iterations, this results in a total of PI i=1 ki ≤I ·N nodes, leading to a space complexity of O(I · N). VI.
