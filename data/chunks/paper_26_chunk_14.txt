DRAGIN [124] discusses a real-time dynamic retrieval mechanism that adapts to the evolving needs of the language model, ensuring that the retrieval strategy remains responsive and aligned with the im- mediate task requirements, thus optimizing the relevance and precision of the retrieved information. In summary, the choice of retrieval strategy within RAG depends on the specific requirements of the application at hand. While basic retrieval strategies offer simplicity and efficiency, iterative retrieval is well-suited for tasks requiring detailed exploration and refinement. Recursive retrieval excels in managing hierarchical information, while adaptive retrieval provides flexibility in dynamic environments. Conditional retrieval ensures strict adherence to predefined criteria, making it indispensable in applications where compliance and specific constraints are critical.
