This suggests a point of diminishing returns where an additional retrieval cycle is more likely to introduce noisy or tangentially related infor- mation that complicates the final synthesis step. • Cost-Benefit Analysis: Each iteration adds a considerable number of API calls and to- kens, increasing both latency and computa- tional cost. The optimal balance of 2-3 it- erations provides the best balance between answer quality and resource consumption. Conversely, on the simpler TriviaQA dataset, the quality rank degrades with each additional it- eration, confirming that for single-hop queries, the initial retrieval is generally sufficient, and further iterations are unnecessary and even detrimental. This analysis confirms that iteration is crucial for complex reasoning, but an unrestrained number of iterations is suboptimal.
