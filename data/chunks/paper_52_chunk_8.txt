The German Zero-Shot Prompt demonstrated more nuanced classification abilities, particularly for intermediate proficiency levels. Next, we observed further substantial improvements in classification accuracy across all proficiency levels for the German Few-Shot Prompt. The confusion matrix revealed a more diagonal pattern, indicating better discrimination between adjacent proficiency levels (see Fig. 1 (c); detailed metrics are provided in appendix C, Table 7)). Especially interesting was the model’s improved handling of extreme proficiency levels. A1 classification showed a precision of 0.833 and recall of 0.600, while C2 classification achieved a precision of 0.629 and recall of 0.880 (mean classification distance of 0.467 highlights this improvement as well). This balanced performance across the proficiency spectrum suggests that the few-shot examples helped the model develop a more nuanced understanding of the characteristics associated with each CEFR level.
